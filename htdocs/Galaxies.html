Galaxies

The Foundation of the Universe

They are one of the largest units of foundation that make up the universe but the truth is that the universe is so big that galaxies are nothing if compared in size. There are over trillions of galaxies scattered around the universe, many still unidentified. We ourselves are living in a spiral galaxy, the Milky Way, and it spans over 100,000 light years in diameter which is about 1,000,000,000,000,000,000 km. Galaxies are formed due to gravity but it is speculated that dark matter is the force that keeps it in shape. Each galaxy has a super massive black hole at its center and each contains billions of trillions of stars.
